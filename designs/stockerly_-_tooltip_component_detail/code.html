What it measures

Measures a company's current share price relative to its per-share earnings (EPS). It indicates how much investors are willing to pay for every $1 of earnings generated.

How to interpret

A high P/E could mean that a stock's price is high relative to earnings and possibly overvalued. Alternatively, it may reflect high growth expectations. Compare this to the sector median (22.1) and the 5-year historical average to identify deviation.
